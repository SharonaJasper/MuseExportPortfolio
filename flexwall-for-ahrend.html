Flexwall for Ahrend

From the University of Twente in collaboration with Ahrend, we got the assignment to design a new piece of office furniture, in compliance with the Ahrend's vision and their existing products.

 

 

The Flexwall is a completely new concept, combining a room divider and flexible workspace in an office setting.

 

The Flexwall's modular design makes it suitable for most office spaces. At least two end modules are needed for the Flexwall, but it can be extended to the desired length with a number of normal modules in between. The end modules are rounded, have storage spaces on the sides and an entrance for the power supply. The power cable can go through the wall to the next modules, resulting in no visible extension cables on the office floor. Moreover, the modularity makes the product more sustainable as a whole, allowing quicker and easier repairs after many years of usage, which extends the product life cycle. If one module may need maintenance or a check up, the rest of the Flexwall does not have to move.

 

The flower pots on top of the wall are easily detachable, enabling a quick change of scenery or look at the inside mechanism. The desks are adjustable in height, giving the user a choice to work sitting or standing in an ergonomically responsible way.

The great advantage of this feature is that the Flexwall could be used for a brief or a longer period, making it a less static workspace.

 

The desks it selves are implemented with an already existing Ahrend PuK, which is a pad for, among other things, wireless phone charging. The idea of adjustable heights could eventually be combined with this Ahrend PuK, allowing the desks to automatically set the height based on a personal profile.

 

Due to the angle between the modules, some desks could be combined to a larger desk. As a result the user may have a larger workspace for himself, or can share it with a coworker, which makes working together a lot more effective. Above these combined desks it is possible to attach whiteboards.
